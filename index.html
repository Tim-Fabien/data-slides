Data Strategy

Foundations for a Modern, AI-Ready Organization

Generic template • Company-agnostic • CC-BY

Purpose: a clean baseline to communicate data vision, operating model, architecture, governance, and value.

Vision

  • Turn data into decisions and AI into outcomes.
  • Make insights and models reliable, fast, and governed.
  • Build a scalable platform that reduces time-to-value.

North Star: measurable impact on growth, efficiency, and customer experience.

Guiding Principles

Value First

  • Business outcomes over vanity metrics
  • Clear KPI ownership
  • “Thin slice” delivery

Platform over Projects

  • Reusable, composable components
  • Self-service by default
  • Automate the boring

Responsible by Design

  • Governance & lineage baked in
  • Privacy & policy as code
  • Human-in-the-loop for AI

Operating Model

Org

  • Central Platform (DE/ML/MLOps)
  • Embedded Analytics in product domains
  • Clear RACI across Data, Product, Eng, Legal

Ways of Working

  • Quarterly bets, monthly reviews
  • Experimentation culture
  • SLAs for data products

Governance & Risk

  • Metadata-first: catalog, lineage, ownership
  • Privacy & Compliance: consent, minimization, retention
  • Policy as Code: access, PII tags, masking
  • AI Controls: evals, guardrails, incident playbooks

Experimentation at Scale

Framework

  • Central assignment & exposure logs
  • Stats (A/B, CUPED, causal)
  • Sequencing & guardrails

Success

  • Lift per 1k users (conversion/retention)
  • Time-to-decision ↓
  • False positives ↓

MLOps Lifecycle

  1. Problem framing & baselines
  2. Data contracts & feature store
  3. Training + evals (offline/online)
  4. Deployment (batch, real-time, streaming)
  5. Monitoring (drift, bias, latency)
  6. Governance (model cards, approvals)

Org & Roles

  • Platform: DE, ML, MLOps, Governance
  • Embedded: Product Analysts / DS in domains
  • COE: Experimentation, Enablement, Education

Principle: centralize complexity, decentralize usage.

Investment Thesis

  • Prioritize platforms that reduce marginal cost of the next use case
  • Bundle governance with delivery to avoid “toothless” compliance
  • Fund durable capabilities; rent the rest

Risks & Mitigations

Tech Risk

  • Vendor lock-in → standards, abstractions
  • Data quality → contracts, tests

AI Risk

  • Bias / hallucinations → evals, guardrails
  • Privacy → PII tagging, masking
